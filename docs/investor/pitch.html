The Problem

Hosts and players are stuck between fragmented tools.

"The game was exploding, and the infrastructure was terrible."
— Jorge Barragan, CEO of The Picklr (61+ locations), Forbes · Feb 2026

For Hosts & Organizers

  • Manual member management across WhatsApp, Excel, Google Forms
  • Tournament brackets break at scale (50 → 200+ players)
  • No unified view across multiple groups or events
  • Referee coordination, court allocation, budget — all in different tools

For Players & Communities

  • No single app to find groups, join tournaments, track progress
  • No persistent identity, history, or rating across events
  • No reason to return weekly — engagement decays after a few sessions
  • No connection between play, health, equipment, and community

The market is fragmented across four tool categories

Tournament software (PB360, Pickleball Brackets, Swish) · Rating systems (DUPR) · Community apps (TeamSnap, Heja, OpenSports) · Facility management (Court Reserve). No one has unified them.

The white space is the entire ecosystem.

The Multi-Role Flywheel

Five roles. Every new role adds value for the others.

1

3

The cross-side network effect

More hosts → more events → more players → more demand for referees and courts → more revenue for stores → more reasons for hosts to stay. Each role makes every other role more valuable. This is the same defensibility mechanism behind Airbnb, Grab, and TikTok.

Single-function competitors cannot replicate cross-side network effects without rebuilding the entire ecosystem.

Unique Value · 01

Health Data — the most valuable longitudinal dataset in racket sports.

Every session generates structured health data. At scale, this becomes a defensible, proprietary asset that no competitor entering later can replicate.

What we capture

  • Calories, steps, heart rate zones, intensity, recovery
  • Session frequency, duration, social context
  • Injury patterns, fitness trajectory, BMI trends
  • Wearable sync (Apple Health, Google Fit, Garmin)

Who values it

  • Insurers — anonymized cohort data on activity & outcomes
  • Healthcare systems — NCD prevention pathways

The Vietnam policy alignment

Vietnam spends ~$20–25B/year on healthcare; non-communicable diseases (cardiovascular, diabetes, obesity) are the largest growth category. The government has set national physical-activity targets. MatchUp directly supports national healthcare policy — opening a pathway for government partnership and subsidized user acquisition.

Competitive Moat

Four layers of defensibility — compounding over time.

01 · First-Mover in Vietnam

No US/EU competitor has invested in Vietnamese localization. Home-court advantage = 12–18 month head start before any competitor can respond meaningfully.

02 · Ecosystem Lock-In

Hosts lose community data. Players lose XP, badges, coins, friends. Referees lose track records. Court owners lose host relationships. Stores lose affiliate history. Switching costs at every layer.

03 · Coin Economy

Once users hold a coin balance, they have a financial reason to stay. Gifting creates social obligation. The TikTok retention mechanic, applied to sports.

04 · Data & AI Compounding

Every session improves matchmaking, health coaching, churn prediction. Late entrants face a permanent data deficit. The moat widens with every user.

No single-function competitor can match this defensibility without rebuilding the entire ecosystem from scratch.
